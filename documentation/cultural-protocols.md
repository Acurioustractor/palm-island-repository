# Cultural Protocols & Data Sovereignty Guidelines
## Palm Island Community Repository

### Indigenous Data Sovereignty Principles

**Indigenous Data Sovereignty** is the right of Indigenous peoples to control data about our communities, cultures, and territories. This includes the right to:
- Own and control data collection, storage, and usage
- Determine how data is used and shared
- Ensure data serves community priorities and values
- Maintain cultural protocols around sensitive information

### Sharing Protocols

#### Before Adding Content:
1. **Permission Check**: Do you have permission to share this story?
2. **Attribution**: Who should be credited for this knowledge/story?
3. **Sensitivity Assessment**: Does this contain sacred or private information?
4. **Community Benefit**: How does sharing this serve the community?

#### Elder Stories - Special Requirements:

### Data Storage & Security

#### Storage Hierarchy (Most to Least Secure):
1. **Elder/Cultural Knowledge**: Encrypted, restricted access, community-controlled
2. **Community-Only Content**: Secure community database
3. **Public Stories**: Standard security with attribution
4. **Historical Archives**: Secure with appropriate metadata

#### Technical Implementation:
- **Primary Storage**: Supabase (secure, Indigenous-controlled)
- **Backup Systems**: Multiple secure locations
- **Access Control**: Role-based permissions
- **Audit Trail**: Track who accesses what content

### Photo and Video Protocols

#### Before Using Images/Videos:
1. **Identify People**: Who is in the photo/video?
2. **Get Permission**: Consent from individuals shown
3. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Any cultural considerations?
4. **Context**: Provide proper context and location
5. **Quality**: Ensure respectful, positive representation

#### Special Considerations:
- **Ceremony/Sacred Sites**: Generally not photographed/shared
- **Children**: Extra care with permissions from parents/guardians
- **Deceased Persons**: Follow cultural protocols for ancestor images
- **Country/Land**: Respect for traditional ownership and sacred sites

### Annual Report Guidelines

#### Community Control:
- Community determines what goes in annual reports
- Stories and data serve community priorities
- No external requirements override community decisions
- Reports reflect community values and aspirations

#### Data Use:
- Only use data that serves the community
- Aggregate sensitive information appropriately
- Always maintain individual privacy
- Focus on strengths and achievements

### Enforcement and Compliance

#### Community Responsibility:
- Everyone has responsibility to follow these protocols
- Cultural advisors available for guidance
- Regular review and updates of protocols
- Training and education for all users

#### Violations:
- Immediate removal of inappropriate content
- Discussion with community leadership
- Additional training if needed
- Potential access restrictions for serious violations
