# Comprehensive Web Research Report: Palm Island & PICC
**Date:** December 2, 2025
**Research Focus:** Publicly available content about Palm Island (Queensland, Australia) and Palm Island Community Company (PICC)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report identifies publicly available digital content about Palm Island and PICC across eight research areas. Key findings include:

- **High-quality historical sources** available through Wikipedia, Queensland Government, and academic institutions
- **Strong PICC documentation** with multiple annual reports (2009-2024) publicly accessible
- **Significant archival materials** through Queensland State Archives, State Library of Queensland, and National Library of Australia
- **Academic research** from James Cook University and other institutions
- **Documentary films** covering both historical events (1957 strike) and contemporary issues (2004 death in custody)
- **Cultural sensitivity required** for all content use, respecting Indigenous data sovereignty principles

**Priority Content for Knowledge Base:**
1. PICC Annual Reports (comprehensive service documentation)
2. Queensland Government community histories
3. State Library of Queensland archives and exhibitions
4. Academic research papers (with permission)
5. Trove digitized newspaper archives

### 2.3 Place Names & Significance

**Key Finding:** "Bwgcolman" (pronounced Bwook-a-mun) means "many tribes - one people" and is the contemporary Aboriginal name for Palm Islanders, reflecting the diverse language groups (57+) forcibly relocated to the island.

**Cultural Sensitivity Note:** Place naming is deeply significant. The dual naming of Palm Island/Bwgcolman reflects both colonial history and Indigenous identity assertion.

### 12.3 Cultural Protocols for Specific Content Types

**Historical Photographs:**
- Even public domain images require community permission
- May depict deceased persons (cultural restrictions)
- May show sacred/restricted practices
- Always consult with Bwgcolman Indigenous Knowledge Centre

**Language Materials:**
- Language is cultural intellectual property
- Contact North Queensland Regional Aboriginal Corporation Language Centre
- Do not use without permission, even for educational purposes

**Stories and Oral Histories:**
- May be restricted to specific family lines or gender
- Storytelling rights belong to specific people/groups
- Recording and republishing requires explicit permission

**Names and Images of Deceased:**
- Some Aboriginal cultures restrict use of names/images of recently deceased
- Always include cultural warning where appropriate
- Consult with family and community

**Sacred Sites and Knowledge:**
- Some information is restricted (gender-specific, initiation-level, etc.)
- Do not publish location information for sacred sites
- Defer to Traditional Owners on what can be shared
