### **3. HULL RIVER HISTORY & ELDER JOURNEYS** ðŸŒ¿ PLANNED
**Historical Context**:
- 1918-1930: Hull River Mission operated
- 1930: Forced removal to Palm Island
- 90+ years of disconnection from traditional lands
- Contemporary: Return journeys, native title claims, cultural revival

**Platform Features**:
- Elder oral history recording (with cultural protocols)
- Traditional place names documentation (Gunnamaru, Bugumanbara)
- Return journey photo/video galleries
- Interactive historical timeline
- Map of traditional places with audio pronunciations
- Native title evidence compilation

**Why It Matters**:
- **Healing**: Reconnection to traditional lands heals intergenerational trauma
- **Justice**: Documentation supports native title claims
- **Identity**: Youth learn about their traditional country
- **Preservation**: Elder knowledge archived before it's lost
- **Sovereignty**: Community controls historical narrative

**Cultural Protocols**:
- Tiered access (public, community, family, restricted, sacred)
- Gender restrictions for certain knowledge
- Elder and family approval workflows
- Technology enforces traditional sharing rules

**Implementation Timeline**: Months 6-9

### **4. THE STATION YOUTH PROJECT** ðŸš€ PLANNED
**The Vision**: 30-hectare property outside Townsville for at-risk youth

**The Need**:
- 40%+ of Queensland detention is Aboriginal youth (3.5% of population)
- Current system: punishment, not healing
- Evidence shows: culture + land + family = effective intervention

**The Model**:
- Residential support (not detention)
- Agricultural programs (horses, gardens, farm work)
- Cultural connection (elder mentorship, traditional practices)
- Family engagement (not removal)
- Education pathways (alternative to mainstream)
- Transition support (reintegration planning)

**Platform Integration**:
```sql
-- Same platform, different organization
The Station â†’ Child org of PICC
- Independent annual reports
- Youth-specific privacy protections
- Outcomes tracking (behavioral, cultural, educational)
- Story collection (youth-controlled sharing)
- Family connection features
- Evidence base for effectiveness
```

**Youth Privacy Approach**:
- Youth control their own stories
- Multiple privacy levels (just me, staff, family, aggregate, public)
- Deidentification for external sharing
- Trauma-informed design (no forced participation)
- Progressive autonomy (more control as they mature)

**Annual Report Power**:
- Prove program effectiveness
- Show culture-based approaches work
- Secure funding through data
- Influence youth justice policy
- Model for other Indigenous youth services

**Implementation Timeline**: Months 9-12, full operation Year 2
